background: >
  The Newt in Somerset (one of Candide's anchor garden partners) wanted a gift membership feature
  ahead of Christmas. Candide had received over 100 support messages from people
  asking how to gift a membership, making the commercial case obvious. The challenge was execution:
  a small team, an immovable Christmas deadline, and a user base that included older adults with
  limited digital confidence who were exactly the audience most likely to give and receive garden
  memberships as gifts.

problems_solutions:
  - problem: Older receivers were worried they wouldn't be able to use a digital gift; they expected something physical and were sceptical of QR codes.
    solution: Added a "preview" link in the purchase flow so gifters could see exactly what their receiver would experience before completing the purchase, reducing the unknown and building confidence in the gift.
  - problem: The existing date picker was designed for near-future selections only, blocking advance gifting and making the feature unusable for anyone buying months ahead.
    solution: Designed a new paginated calendar UI supporting gift delivery dates months in advance, validated against three concept directions before landing on the most intuitive approach.
  - problem: Gifters were worried about buying a duplicate membership for someone who was already a member, or not knowing how to handle a joint membership.
    solution: Resolved edge cases (existing members, multi-holder memberships, access variants) without adding complexity to the primary flow, keeping the majority path clean.
  - problem: The digital gift experience felt transactional, with no personalisation that would make it feel like a real gift rather than a product code.
    solution: Added a personalised message step at checkout and redesigned the receiver email to lead with garden imagery and a description of what the year's membership would include.
  - problem: The flow needed to work for users aged 65+ with lower digital literacy, without compromising the experience for everyone else.
    solution: Applied WCAG AAA standards, larger touch targets, increased spacing, and plain-language copy, validated with older users through usertesting.com, achieving an 80% "very easy" rating from that group.

takeaways:
  - The feature generated £32k in revenue across 505 gift orders in its first three months, entirely new revenue from a flow that previously didn't exist.
  - 92% of purchasers added a personal message, validating the decision to invest in the personalisation step as a core part of the flow.
  - 80% of over-65 year olds rated the receiving experience as very easy, strong evidence that accessible design doesn't compromise conversion for other groups.
  - 13 other potential garden venue customers initiated partnership discussions with Candide after launch, creating pipeline beyond the original feature scope.
